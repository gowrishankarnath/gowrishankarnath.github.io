Process involved in writing Course Outcomes (COs) and mapping of Course Outcomes with Program Outcomes (POs) for Course Articulation Matrix

Date Fri 24 May 2019 Tags Course Outcomes / Program Outcomes / CO / PO / NBA / Accreditation / Course / SAR / OBE

Status: draft

Recently, me along with my colleagues Prof. Veena A. (Dept. of CSE, Dr.AIT) and Prof. Pushpaveni H (Dept. of CSE, Dr.AIT), were assigned with the task to come up with a process for writing the Course Outcomes (COs) for a course and the mapping of the Course Outcomes with Program Outcomes (POs) for Course Articulation Matrix.

National Board of Accreditation

National Board of Accreditation (NBA) is responsible for accreditation of higher education institutions in India. NBA is also a full member of the Washington Accord [provide link]. NBA accredits programmes and not the institutions.

If an institution is going for NBA accreditation, then in Criterion 3.1 of Self Assessment Report (SAR) of NBA document, you are explicitly asked to mention the CO - PO mapping for a minimum of 8 core courses.

So before moving on for NBA accreditation, every department should make sure that their COs are well defined and serves the purpose of Outcome Based Education (OBE). Institutions adapting OBE try to bring changes to the curriculum by dynamically adopting to the requirements of the different stakeholders like Students, Parents, Industry Personnel and Recruiters.

Tier - 1 Institutions consists of all IITs, NITs, Central Universities, State Universities and Autonomous Institutions.

Tier - 2 Institutions consists of affiliated colleges of Universities.

Tier - 1 institutions can also claim the benefits as per the Washington Accord.

What is Outcome Based Education (OBE)?

Institutions adapting OBE try to bring changes to curriculum by dynamically adapting to the requirements of the different stakeholders like Students, Parents, Industry Personnel and Recruiters. OBE is all about feedback and outcomes.

Why OBE?

1) International recognition and Global employment opportunities 2) More employable and innovative graduates with professional and soft skills, social responsibility and ethics 3) Better visibility and reputation of technical institution among stakeholders 4) Improving the commitment and involvement of all stakeholders 5) Enabling graduates to excel in their profession and career accomplishments 6) Preparing graduates with leadership positions and challenging technology development opportunities

Benefits of OBE for Faculty Members

Faculty members are referred to as Change of Agents in OBE.

1) Teaching will become a far more creative and innovative career 2) Faculty members will no longer feel the pressure of having to be the “source of all knowledge” 3) Faculty members shape the thinking and vision of students towards a course.

Program Outcomes (POs)

1) POs describe what students should know and be able to do at the end of the programme. 2) POs are to be in line with the graduate attributes of International Engineering Alliance. 3) POs are to be specific, measurable and achievable.

NBA has defined 12 POs and you need not define and it common for all the institutions.

In the syllabus book, all the Professors should clearly mention the course objectives and course outcomes along with CO-PO mapping for a given course.

Course Outcomes (COs)

Course outcomes are the statements of what a student should know, understand and/or be able to demonstrate after completion of a course.

  1. The COs must state the major skills, knowledge, attitude or ability that students will acquire.
  2. COs should be expressed in terms of measurable and/or observable behaviors.
  3. COs should be agreed upon by the faculty in a program and should drive program outcomes.
  4. All the courses in the department should have a uniform number of COs.
  5. The COs should be written by Professors who have demonstrated considerable expertise in that course and then identify the textbook for the course.
  6. The syllabus book should have CO-PO mapping of all the courses and along with their values.
  7. There should be 6 COs according to the NBA.
  8. For POs from 1-6 the values can be defined based on the action verbs itself.
  9. From PO7 to PO12 the program coordinator has to set the Blooms cognitive level. Based on this level, all the instructors have to choose the values.

Course outcomes should begin with an action verb.

Blooms Taxonomy

Bloom’s taxonomy is considered as the global language for education. Bloom’s Taxonomy is frequently used by teachers in writing course outcomes as it provides a ready made structure and list of action verbs. More information about Bloom’s taxonomy can be found here.

NOTE:

  1. The first six POs are purely of technical in nature, while the other POs are non-technical.
  2. For the theory courses, while composing COs, you need to restrict yourself between Level 1 to Level 4. Again, if it is a programming course, restrict yourself between Level 1 to Level 3 but for the other courses, you can go up to Level 4.
  3. For the laboratory courses, while composing COs, you need to restrict yourself between Level 1 and Level 5.
  4. Only for Mini-project and Main project, you may extend up to Level 6 while composing COs.
  5. For a given course, the course in-charge has to involve all the other Professors who teach that course and ask them to come up with the CO-PO mapping. The course in-charge has to take the average value of all of these CO-PO mappings and finalize the values. Ensure none of the Professors who are handling the particular course discuss with each other while marking the CO-PO values.
  6. If you want to match your COs with non-technical POs, then correlate the action verbs used in the course COs with the thumb rule given in the table and map the values. (Applies only for mapping COs to non-technical POs)

Tips for Assigning the values while mapping COs to POs.

  1. Select action verbs for a CO from different blooms levels based on the importance of the particular CO for the given course.
  2. Stick to single action verbs while composing COs but you may go for multiple action verbs if the need arises.
  3. Values to CO-PO (technical POs in particular) matrix can be assigned by
  4. Judging the importance of the particular CO in relation to the POs. If the CO matches strongly with a particular PO criterion then assign 3, if it matches moderately then assign 2 or if the match is low then assign 1 else mark with ” - ” symbol.
  5. If an action verb used in a CO is repeated at multiple Bloom’s levels, then you need to judge which Blooms level is the best fit for that action verb.

Few Clarifications

Course: A semester consists of 5 to 6 theory courses and 2 to 3 laboratory courses. Course is a unit of teaching. We Indians tend to use the term Subject instead of Course. Please avoid the use of term “Subject”. Use the term Course instead of subject. The term Programme is different from Department. Programme refers to individual degrees like B.E., B.Tech, M.Tech and Ph.D. All the degrees together form the department.

Programme: Cohesive arrangement of courses, co-curricular and extra-curricular activities to accomplish predetermined objectives leading to the award of a degree.

Degree: Academic award conferred upon a student upon successful completion of a programme. For example, B.E. Degree Programme, B.Tech Degree Programme, M.E. Degree Programme and M.Tech Degree Programme.

Output and Outcome: Let me explain these two terms with an example. If you produce Ph.D students then it is Output. If the Ph.D students are able to get funds and publish papers then it is Outcome.
