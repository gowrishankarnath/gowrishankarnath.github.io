Positive Approaches Towards Successful Implementation of Outcome Based Education (OBE) from India Context

Date Fri 03 May 2019 Tags NBA / OBE / Education / Accreditation

This article is drawn from my personal experience in implementing the Outcome Based Education (OBE) at our institution and also through discussion with my esteemed colleagues from other Institutions. I also like to inform you that “What might have worked in our Institution, may not work in your organization”. So, take the below information with a pinch of salt and do not consider it as a gospel. OBE is all about feedback and outcomes for our actions.

National Board of Accreditation

National Board of Accreditation (NBA) is responsible for accreditation of higher education institutions in India. NBA is also a full member of the Washington Accord [provide link]. NBA accredits programmes and not the institutions.

Higher Education Institutions in India are classified into two categories by NBA

Tier - 1 Institutions consists of all IITs, NITs, Central Universities, State Universities and Autonomous Institutions.

Tier - 2 Institutions consists of affiliated colleges of Universities.

Tier - 1 institutions can also claim the benefits as per the Washington Accord.

Why OBE? 1) International recognition and Global employment opportunities 2) More employable and innovative graduates with professional and soft skills, social responsibility and ethics 3) Better visibility and reputation of technical institution among stakeholders 4) Improving the commitment and involvement of all stakeholders 5) Enabling graduates to excel in their profession and career accomplishments 6) Preparing graduates with leadership positions and challenging technology development opportunities

Benefits of OBE -Teacher

Faculty members are referred to as Change of Agents in OBE.

1) Teaching will become a far more creative and innovative career 2) Lecturers will no longer feel the pressure of having to be the “source of all knowledge” 3) Producing thinking, caring students

You should define Institute Vision and Mission to comply with the tradition, culture and ethos of your institution.

Institution Vision:

Vision is a picture of the future you seek to create, described in the present tense, as if it were happening now. It shows where we want to go, and what we will be like when we get there.

Institution Mission:

Mission statement defines what an institution is, why the institution exists, its reason for being. It defines what are we here to do together

Department Vision

The vision and mission of the department should be correlated with the mission and vision of the institution. More focused on the theme area and can be derived from SWOT analysis.

Department Mission

A mission statement might include a brief history and philosophy of the academic programme, the type of students to be served, the academic environment and primary focus of the curriculum, faculty roles, the contributions to and connections with the community, the role of research.

A senior professor who has undergone NBA training should be designated as NBA coordinator for a programme.

Few Clarifications:

Course: A semester consists of 5 to 6 theory courses and 2 to 3 laboratory courses. Course is a unit of teaching. We Indians tend to use the term Subject instead of Course. Please avoid the use of term “Subject”.

Programme: Cohesive arrangement of courses, co-curricular and extra-curricular activities to accomplish predetermined objectives leading to the award of a degree.

Degree: Academic award conferred upon a student upon successful completion of a programme. For example, B.E. Degree Programme, B.Tech Degree Programme, M.E. Degree Programme and M.Tech Degree Programme.

Output and Outcome: Let me explain these two terms with an example. If you produce Ph.D students then it is Output. If the Ph.D students are able to get funds and publish papers then it is Outcome.

Most of the professors tend to identify the textbook and then write the course outcomes. But it should be the other way around. The course expert should define the Course Outcomes for a particular course and then select the books that satisfy the course outcomes.

NBA has defined 12 POs and you need not define and it common for all the institutions.

What is Outcome Based Education (OBE)?

Institutions adapting OBE try to bring changes to curriculum by dynamically adapting to the requirements of the different stakeholders like Students, Parents, Industry Personnel and Recruiters. OBE is all about feedback and outcomes.

Four levels of outcomes from OBE are:

1) Course Outcomes

2) Program Outcomes

3) Program Educational Outcomes (PEOs)

4) Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)

Course Outcomes 1) The course outcomes must state the major skills, knowledge, attitude or ability that students will acquire. 2) Course outcomes should be expressed in terms of measurable and/or observable behaviors 3) Course Outcomes should be agreed upon by the faculty in a program and should drive program outcomes.

Course outcomes should begin with an action verb (e.g., write, install, solve, and apply)

Programme Educational Objectives (PEOs) 1) PEOs are broad statements that describe the career and professional accomplishments that the programme is preparing graduates to accomplish after 3 to 5 years of graduation. 2) PEOs should be measurable, appropriate, realistic, and achievable. 3) PEOs addresses needs of the stakeholders

Guidelines for the PEOs 1) PEOs should be consistent with the mission of the Institution 2) The number of PEOs should be manageable 3) PEOs should be achievable by the programme 4) PEOs should be specific to the programme and not too broad

Programme Outcomes (POs) 1) POs describe what students should know and be able to do at the end of the programme. 2) POs are to be in line with the graduate attributes of IEA. 3) POs are to be specific, measurable and achievable. 4) POs transform the PEOs into specific student performance and behaviors that demonstrate student learning and skill development

Use the term Course instead of subject. The term Programme is different from Department. Programme refers to individual degrees like B.E., B.Tech, M.Tech and Ph.D. All the degrees together form the department.

For example, if you produce Ph.D students then it is output, but if the Ph.D students are able to publish the papers and get fund then it is called as Outcome.

While writing COs, first the COs should be defined by the course expert and then identify the contents and the textbook for the course.

Key Components of OBE are

1) Vision and Mission of the Department

2) Program Outcomes (POs)

3) Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

4) Course Outcomes (COs)

Vision is an aspiration and long term goal. Vision should be defined in plane English and be very clear. Don’t use jargons. In order to achieve the vision a set of activities needs to be carried out. This is referred to as Mission statement. Mission statements should start with action words.

PEOs are not for the department but for each programmes like B.E., B.Tech, M.Tech.

PEOs should be defined by the Program Coordinator or Department HEAD. PEOs are an promise by the HOS to the aspiring students about what they will achieve once they join the programme. PEO assessment is not compulsory in India as it is quite difficult to measure in Indian context. Assessors usually do not ask for PEO assessment. PEOs are about professional and career accomplishment after 4 to 5 years of graduation.

POs are synthetic but PEOs are actual. PO is like getting driving license but actual driving is PEO.

Write about Self Assessment Report

Need and functionality of Pedagogy group

  1. Need to have a Pedagogy group.
  2. Appoint any two young assistant professors who can visit other colleges, go for training and workshops and digest their best practices.
  3. These assistant professors should be in a position to train other teachers, implement pedagogoy practices in their department and impress upon other professors to follow the best practices.

  4. These assistant professors should teach the best practices to other professors in the department.

PEOs can be written from three different perspectives.

1) Career

2) Technical Competency

3) Behavior

Don’t use technical terms while defining PEOs. It will be read by prospective students who wants to join a programme. Three to five PEOs are recommended.

NBA says both Graduate Attributes (GAs) and POs are same.

Feedback is very important in OBE.

Feedback for all the courses 1) From the students at the end of the semester itself. Feedback about the programme 1) From the parents. 2) From the employees.

Blooms Taxonomy

Bloom’s Taxonomy of Educational Objectives

Bloom’s Taxonomy is frequently used by teachers in writing course outcomes as it provides a ready made structure and list of verbs.

Course outcomes • Course outcomes are statements of what a student should know, understand and/or be able to demonstrate after completion of a course

Blooms taxonomy is considered as the global language for education.

Three dimensions of learning are

1) Knowledge (K)

2) Skills (S)

3) Analytics (A)

KSA

What is rubric? A scoring guide used to evaluate the quality of students’ constructed responses

Advantages Set focus on stated objectives Set a common framework and language for assessment Grading consistency among students Grading consistency among teachers Enables peer review Clear instruction to students on intended outcomes Diagnosis of specific strengths and weaknesses

Rubrics • Rubrics is set of performance indicators which define and describe the important component of the work being completed • Information to/about individual student competence (Analytic) – Communicate expectations – Diagnosis for purpose of improvement and feedback • Overall examination of the status of the performance of a group of students? (Holistic)

Rubric Design for laboratory programs. In Indian universities, there is a trend of allocating marks based on face value of the students. Usually, a professor sees a student and allocates marks based on his opinion about the student. This tends to have negative effect on the student. No transparency in this approach as one can’t say what was the marks breakup. We can overcome these kind of problems by adopting Rubrics Design for laboratory courses.

Advantages:

1) Sets focus on the stated objectives

2) Sets a common framework and language for assessment

3) Grading consistency among the students and faculty members

4) Enables peer review

5) Clear Instructions to students on the intended outcomes

6) Diagnosing specific Strengths and Weaknesses among students.

Always distribute the rubrics to the students so that they do understand how they are accessed.

In the syllabus book clearly mention the course objectives and course outcomes along with CO-PO mapping.
